Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 25, 2026

Cynda ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes what data we collect, why we collect it, and how it is handled — both when you use the Cynda web application and when you use the Cynda Slack bot.

1. Data We Collect

Account data:

  • Email address and name — provided when creating an account.
  • Password (hashed) — stored as a one-way hash; never stored in plain text.
  • Google OAuth tokens — access and refresh tokens used to query BigQuery on your behalf, when you authorize via Google OAuth.

Through the Cynda Slack bot:

  • Slack workspace ID and team name — stored when you install the Cynda bot to your workspace.
  • Slack bot token — stored to allow Cynda to send responses to your workspace.
  • Natural-language questions — the messages you send to the bot.
  • Generated SQL and summaries — the SQL and plain-English answer produced in response to your question, stored in conversation history.

Database connections:

  • BigQuery project IDs — stored when you configure a BigQuery connection.
  • PostgreSQL connection strings — stored when you configure a PostgreSQL connection. Connection strings may include credentials and are stored encrypted.
  • Service account credentials — JSON service account keys you provide for BigQuery access, stored encrypted.

We do not store the contents of your database tables. Query results are fetched to answer your question and are not persisted on our servers.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Account data — to authenticate you and provide access to the service.
  • Slack workspace data — to route responses to the correct workspace and maintain the bot installation.
  • Questions and conversation history — to operate the service, provide follow-up context across messages, and improve query accuracy.
  • Generated SQL — logged to detect errors, audit usage, and improve the service.
  • Database connection details — stored solely to execute queries on your behalf. Never shared with third parties.
  • OAuth tokens — used exclusively to execute BigQuery queries on your behalf. Stored so you do not need to re-authorize on every query.

We do not sell, rent, or share any of your data with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

3. Data Retention

  • Account and conversation data — retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Session tokens — expire automatically after 30 days.
  • OAuth state tokens — automatically deleted after 10 minutes.
  • Password reset tokens — automatically deleted after use or expiry.
  • Slack workspace data — retained until you uninstall the Cynda app from your workspace or delete your account.

Upon account deletion, all associated data is permanently removed, including conversation history, database connections, OAuth tokens, and Slack workspace records.

4. AI and LLM Data Processing

Cynda uses AI to convert your natural-language questions into SQL queries and to summarize query results. The following data is sent to our AI provider:

  • Your natural-language question.
  • The schema (column names and types) of your database tables — not the data itself.
  • Up to 100 rows of query results, to generate a plain-English summary.

AI is provided by Google Gemini 2.0 Flash via OpenRouter. On the paid API tier, Google does not use your prompts or responses to train its models. OpenRouter does not train models on user inputs. Cynda never uses your data to train AI models.

AI-generated responses may contain errors or inaccuracies. Always review generated SQL before acting on its results.

5. Data Storage and Security

All data is stored in a managed PostgreSQL database hosted on Railway (railway.app) in the United States. Database credentials, service account keys, and OAuth tokens are stored encrypted at rest. Access to the database is restricted to the Cynda service and its operators.

All data in transit is protected by TLS 1.2 or higher. We apply industry-standard security practices to protect your data against unauthorized access or disclosure.

6. Third-Party Services and Sub-processors

Cynda uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • Slack — to receive messages and send responses via the Slack API.
  • Railway — API hosting and managed PostgreSQL database (United States).
  • OpenRouter — routes LLM requests to AI model providers (United States).
  • Google Cloud — Gemini 2.0 Flash for AI inference; BigQuery for query execution on your data (United States / Global).

For the full list, see our Sub-processors page. Each sub-processor operates under its own privacy policy and data processing agreement.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction or deletion of your data at any time.
  • Revoke your Google OAuth authorization — this deletes your stored tokens and disables BigQuery access until you re-authorize.
  • Uninstall the Cynda Slack app from your workspace at any time.
  • Export or request a copy of your data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@cynda.tech. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Data Deletion

To request deletion of your account and all associated data, email support@cynda.tech from the address associated with your account. We will permanently delete your data within 30 days of receiving a verified request. Deletion covers all records including conversation history, database connection credentials, OAuth tokens, and Slack workspace data.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of Cynda after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact

For any questions or requests regarding this Privacy Policy, reach out at support@cynda.tech.
